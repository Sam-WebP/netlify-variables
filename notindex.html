How much do I need to invest into my ads?

Your ad investment depends on several factors, including your industry, the geographic location you're targeting, and the competitiveness of the keywords you aim to target. Other considerations may include your campaign goals and the current market conditions.

While we excel in optimising campaigns to keep costs manageable, it's advisable to allocate between $1,500 to $3,000 per month to your marketing budget. This range allows for a robust strategy that can adapt to market dynamics and effectively reach your target audience.

What happens after the first month?

If you're pleased with the initial results, you have the option to continue with our services.

The subsequent three months are crucial, as this period involves significant adjustments to the campaigns. During this time, Google's algorithms learn and adapt to better target customers most suitable for your business.

Our ongoing service fee is approximately $400 per month for continuous management and optimisation of your Google Ads campaign. Note that this fee may vary, higher or lower, based on the level of maintenance and resources required for your specific campaign needs.

What if I decide not to continue after the first month?

You have no obligation to continue with our services after the first month. If you choose not to proceed, there will be no charge for the work completed during the trial period.

Should you choose to discontinue, access to the professionally designed landing page we created will be revoked. However, if you wish to retain it, you can purchase the landing page for a one-time fee of $2,000.

It's important to note that to truly gauge the effectiveness of Google Ads, a period of up to 3 months is often necessary. This time allows the algorithm to accurately identify and target the most suitable audience for your business. Ending the campaign prematurely might mean missing out on the full potential of your Google Ads strategy, akin to 'shooting yourself in the foot'. A three-month commitment can provide a more comprehensive understanding of the campaign's true impact.

How do you define 'quality leads' and 'satisfaction'?

Quality leads are potential customers who show a genuine interest in your products or services, as indicated by engagement metrics. Satisfaction refers to your contentment with the number and quality of leads generated.

Is there a risk of my ads not being profitable for my business?

Yes, there's an inherent risk in any advertising, including Google Ads, where profitability isn't guaranteed.

Calculating the exact profitability can be challenging. Prospects might see your ad today but only convert months later, making immediate ROI measurement difficult.

Each new client also brings potential word-of-mouth referrals, enhancing long-term value. Furthermore, profitability is influenced by the Lifetime Value (LTV) of each customer.

While we strive to minimize risks through strategic planning and optimisation, it's vital to understand these complexities and maintain realistic expectations for your ad campaigns.
